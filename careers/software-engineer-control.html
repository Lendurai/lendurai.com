Software Engineer, Control Systems

Tallinn, Estonia (On-site)

About the company and the role

At Lendurai, we enable unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) to operate independently of global navigation satellite systems and radio control, using computer vision and machine learning. We partner with multiple UAV manufacturers to deliver cost-effective capabilities to public sector and defence customers. Our team has deep experience in aerospace engineering, defence tech, and autonomous systems, with backgrounds at companies like Bolt (EE), Open Cosmos (UK), and Starship Technologies (EE).

As a Control Systems Engineer, you will be responsible for developing robust flight control systems that enable UAVs to operate autonomously in GNSS-denied environments. You'll work at the intersection of classical control theory and modern machine learning approaches to create reliable, adaptive control systems.

What you will be doing

  • Design and implement flight control systems for autonomous UAV operations
  • Develop algorithms for attitude control, trajectory planning, and state estimation
  • Create robust control solutions that work in GNSS-denied environments
  • Integrate computer vision and sensor fusion inputs into control loops
  • Implement and tune controllers for different UAV platforms and configurations
  • Work with hardware teams to validate control systems on real platforms
  • Develop testing frameworks for control system validation

We are looking for

Required skills:

  • Strong background in control theory and robotics
  • Experience implementing real-time control systems
  • Proficiency in C++ and Python
  • Understanding of state estimation and sensor fusion
  • Experience with ROS (Robot Operating System)
  • Strong mathematical foundation in linear algebra and optimization

Nice to have:

  • Experience with UAV flight control systems (PX4, ArduPilot)
  • Knowledge of modern control techniques (MPC, adaptive control)
  • Background in computer vision or machine learning
  • Experience with embedded systems and real-time constraints
  • Understanding of aerodynamics and flight mechanics

Additional skills that are advantageous:

  • Experience with Kalman filtering and state estimation
  • Knowledge of optimization techniques for real-time control
  • Understanding of system identification and parameter estimation
  • Experience with hardware-in-the-loop testing

  • Opportunity to work on cutting-edge autonomous systems
  • Access to state-of-the-art testing facilities
  • Professional development opportunities
  • Health insurance
  • Regular team events
